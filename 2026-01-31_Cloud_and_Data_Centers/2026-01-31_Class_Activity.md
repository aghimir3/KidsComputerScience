# Class Activity: Cloud Explorer (Instructor-Led)

**Date:** January 31, 2026
**Topic:** The Cloud & Data Centers
**Duration:** ~40-50 minutes
**Format:** Instructor demonstrates via MS Teams screenshare, students follow along remotely

---

## Overview

This is an instructor-led demonstration via MS Teams screenshare where you show students real cloud infrastructure, data centers, and AI services. Students watch your screen remotely and can follow along on their own devices if they want. The goal is to demystify "the cloud" by showing them the real, physical places where their data lives.

**Remote Teaching Tips:**
- Share your entire screen or a specific browser window via MS Teams
- Keep your browser zoomed in (125-150%) so students can read text clearly
- Pause at each visual to give students time to absorb details
- Encourage students to ask questions in chat or unmute

---

## Demo 1: What is "The Cloud"? (5 minutes)

### What to Show

Open your browser and show:

1. **Google Drive** (drive.google.com)
   - "When you save a file here, where does it actually go?"
   - "It doesn't float in the sky - it goes to a real building!"

2. **Quick visual** - Draw or show a simple diagram:
   ```
   Your Device → Internet → Data Center (servers) → Back to you
   ```

### Key Point to Make
> "The cloud is just someone else's computer - specifically, thousands of computers in huge buildings called data centers."

---

## Demo 2: Google Data Center Tour (15 minutes)

### What to Show

**Step 1: Google's Data Center Map**
1. Go to: `https://datacenters.google/locations/`
2. Show the interactive map
3. Click on a few locations (The Dalles, Oregon is a good one)
4. Point out: "Look how many there are worldwide!"

**Step 2: Google's Photo Gallery**
1. Go to: `https://datacenters.google/discover-more/photo-gallery/`
2. Scroll through and show:
   - **Server aisles** - "These are the actual computers storing your data"
   - **Colored pipes** - "These carry water for cooling - servers get HOT"
   - **Network rooms** - "This is where all the internet cables connect"
   - **Cooling towers** - "Data centers use as much electricity as small cities"

**Step 3: YouTube Video Tour (Optional)**
- Search: "Google Data Center Tour"
- Play 2-3 minutes of an official tour video
- Point out security, scale, and cooling systems

### Things to Point Out
- "See those blinking lights? Each one is processing someone's data right now"
- "The pipes are color-coded - pink for hot water, blue for cold"
- "Notice the security - guards, cameras, locked doors everywhere"

## Demo 3: The Big Three Cloud Providers (10 minutes)

### What to Show

Open three tabs and show each provider's page:

**1. Amazon Web Services (AWS)**
- Go to: `aws.amazon.com`
- "This is the BIGGEST cloud provider - about 33% of the entire cloud market"
- "Netflix, Twitch, and NASA all run on AWS"

**2. Microsoft Azure**
- Go to: `azure.microsoft.com`
- "Second biggest - powers Xbox Live, LinkedIn, and Office 365"
- "This is where ChatGPT runs!"

**3. Google Cloud**
- Go to: `cloud.google.com`
- "Third biggest - powers Spotify, Snapchat, and of course all Google services"

### Quick Comparison
| Provider | Market Share | Famous Users |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| AWS | ~33% | Netflix, Twitch, NASA |
| Azure | ~22% | Xbox, ChatGPT, LinkedIn |
| Google Cloud | ~10% | Spotify, Snapchat, YouTube |

---

## Demo 4: AI + Cloud Connection (10 minutes)

### What to Show

**Step 1: Show ChatGPT**
1. Go to: `chat.openai.com`
2. Type a question and show the response
3. Explain: "Every time I send a message, it travels to Microsoft's data centers, gets processed by thousands of GPUs, and comes back - all in seconds!"

**Step 2: Show Other AI Chatbots** (if time permits)
- **Gemini** (gemini.google.com) - "Runs on Google Cloud"
- **Claude** (claude.ai) - "Runs on AWS and Google Cloud"
- **Grok** (x.com/grok) - "Runs on xAI's own Colossus supercomputer in Memphis"

**Step 3: Why AI Needs the Cloud**
Explain:
- "AI models like GPT-4 are HUGE - too big for any single computer"
- "ChatGPT needs thousands of special chips called GPUs working together"
- "That's why you need internet to use it - your question travels to a data center and back"

### Fun Fact to Share
> "xAI's Colossus data center in Memphis has over 100,000 GPUs - and uses as much electricity as a small city!"

---

## Demo 5: Cloud vs Local Storage (5 minutes)

### What to Show

**Show both side by side:**

1. **Local Storage** - Open File Explorer, show "This PC"
   - "This is storage ON your computer"
   - "Works without internet, but if you lose your laptop, it's gone"

2. **Cloud Storage** - Open Google Drive or OneDrive
   - "This is storage in a data center"
   - "Need internet, but accessible from ANY device"

### Live Demo
1. Create a quick text file in Google Drive
2. Open your phone (or another browser tab)
3. Show the same file appearing there
4. "See? The file traveled to Google's servers and now I can see it anywhere!"

---

## Wrap-Up: Key Takeaways (5 minutes)

Summarize what you showed them:

1. **"The Cloud" = Real buildings** with thousands of servers
2. **Data Centers** are huge facilities with cooling, security, and backup power
3. **Three big companies** (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) run most of the internet
4. **AI needs the cloud** - ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude all run on data center servers
5. **Your data travels** to these buildings every time you use cloud services

## Instructor Notes

### Remote Teaching Tips
- Check that screenshare is working before starting demos
- Verbally describe what you're clicking/showing for anyone with lag
- Pause after switching tabs to let everyone's screen catch up
- Ask "Can everyone see this?" when showing important visuals
- Use MS Teams reactions/polls to check engagement

### Pacing Tips
- Don't rush the photo gallery - let students absorb the scale
- The AI demo is usually the most engaging part
- If running short on time, skip the YouTube video

### Common Student Reactions
- "Wait, so Google can see my files?" - Yes, but they have strict privacy policies
- "What if the data center burns down?" - They have backups in multiple locations
- "Is my Fortnite data in the cloud?" - Yes! Game progress is saved on cloud servers

### Transition to Classwork
After this demo, students will do their own research in the classwork:
- Finding data center locations
- Researching cloud providers
- Answering questions about what they learned
