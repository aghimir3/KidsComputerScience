==================== SLIDE 2 ====================
What is a Network?
Think of it like a FRIENDSHIP GROUP!
A network is when computers are connected so they can talk to each other and share things.
Just like you and your friends pass notes in class, computers send messages through cables or Wi-Fi!
Fun Fact: Your school probably has hundreds of computers all connected in one big network!
💻 ↔️ 💻
Computers talking to each other!

==================== SLIDE 3 ====================
What is an IP Address?
It's like your HOME ADDRESS!
Every computer has a special number called an IP Address. It tells other computers where to find it!
Just like the mailman needs your address to deliver letters, computers need IP addresses to send messages!
Example: 192.168.1.25
IP = Internet Protocol

==================== SLIDE 4 ====================
What is a Gateway?
It's like the FRONT DOOR of your network!
A Gateway is like a security guard at the entrance of a building. It checks who comes in and who goes out!
When your computer wants to visit a website, it asks the gateway: "Can you help me get to the internet?"
Usually: Your router at home is your gateway!

==================== SLIDE 5 ====================
What is DNS?
It's like a PHONE BOOK for the internet!
DNS stands for Domain Name System. It translates website names into IP addresses!
Imagine having to remember 142.250.80.46 instead of google.com - that would be so hard!
DNS helps you type easy names instead of hard numbers!
How DNS Works:
1. You type: www.google.com
⬇️
2. DNS looks it up in the "phone book"
⬇️
3. DNS finds: 142.250.80.46
⬇️
4. You arrive at Google! ✓

What is a Router?
It's like a TRAFFIC POLICE OFFICER!
A Router directs internet traffic to the right place, just like a traffic cop tells cars which way to go!
It makes sure the video you're watching goes to YOUR tablet, not your sister's phone!
At home: The box with blinking lights that gives you Wi-Fi!

==================== SLIDE 7 ====================
Wi-Fi vs Ethernet Cable
📶 Wi-Fi
Like talking through the AIR!
• Uses invisible radio waves
• No cables needed!
• Can walk around with your device
• Sometimes slower than cables
Great for: phones, tablets, laptops
🔌 Ethernet Cable
Like a HIGHWAY for data!
• Uses a special cable
• Super fast connection
• Very reliable - doesn't drop
• Device must stay plugged in
Great for: desktop computers, gaming

==================== SLIDE 8 ====================
What is a Subnet Mask?
It's like NEIGHBORHOOD BOUNDARIES!
A Subnet Mask tells your computer which other computers are in the same "neighborhood" (network).
Just like your ZIP code tells you which houses are in your area!
Common example: 255.255.255.0
This means "we're all in the same neighborhood!"

How Data Travels: Packets!
It's like sending a PUZZLE in the mail!
When you send a picture or message, it gets broken into tiny pieces called PACKETS.
Each packet travels to the destination, then they all get put back together!
Why? Small packets travel faster and are easier to fix if something goes wrong!

==================== SLIDE 10 ====================
What is Ping?
It's like playing MARCO POLO!
Ping is a way to check if another computer is there and responding!
Your computer says "HELLO!" and waits to see if the other computer says "I'M HERE!"
It also tells you how fast the response came back (in milliseconds)!

"Hello!"
➡️
⬅️
"I'm here!"

Result: Reply in 25ms ✓
Lower number = faster connection!

==================== SLIDE 11 ====================
The ipconfig Command
Like asking "What's MY address?"
ipconfig shows you all the network information about YOUR computer!
It tells you your IP address, gateway, and other cool info!
We'll try this together! It's a real command you can type!
Command Prompt
C:\> ipconfig
Windows IP Configuration
Ethernet adapter:
IPv4 Address: 192.168.1.25
Subnet Mask: 255.255.255.0
Default Gateway: 192.168.1.1
Your computer's "ID card" for the network!
