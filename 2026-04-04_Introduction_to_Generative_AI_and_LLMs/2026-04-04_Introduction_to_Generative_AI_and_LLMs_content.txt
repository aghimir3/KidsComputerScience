Part 2: The Transformer Era
2017: "Attention Is All You Need"
The paper that changed everything
The Problem Before
Previous models (RNNs, LSTMs) processed text sequentially - one word at a time. This was slow and struggled with long-range dependencies.
The Breakthrough
Transformers process all words simultaneously using 'self-attention' - allowing the model to see relationships across the entire input at once.
Why It Mattered:
• Massively parallel = much faster training
• Better at understanding context
• Enabled scaling to billions of parameters
This architecture is the foundation of GPT, BERT, Claude, and every modern LLM.

==================== SLIDE 7 ====================
Part 2: The Transformer Era
2018: GPT and BERT Are Born
OpenAI's GPT-1
June 2018 | 117M parameters
Generative Pre-trained Transformer. Designed to generate text by predicting the next word.
Key Characteristics:
• Decoder-only architecture
• Left-to-right text generation
• Autoregressive: predicts next token
• Excels at text generation, completion
Became: GPT-2 → GPT-3 → ChatGPT → GPT-4
Google's BERT
October 2018 | 340M parameters
Bidirectional Encoder. Designed to understand text meaning and context.
Key Characteristics:
• Encoder-only architecture
• Bidirectional context understanding
• Masked language modeling
• Excels at classification, Q&A
Powers: Google Search, sentiment analysis, NER
GPT lineage dominates today's LLMs, but BERT-style models power many search and classification tasks

Part 3: How LLMs Work
Training: How LLMs Learn
1
Pre-training
Learn language patterns from massive text data.
Scale: Trillions of tokens
2
Fine-tuning
Specialize on curated examples and instructions.
Scale: Millions of examples
3
RLHF
Learn human preferences for helpfulness and safety.
Scale: Thousands of comparisons
The Core Idea: Next Token Prediction
"The quick brown" → fox   (Predicted)
By predicting billions of 'next words,' the model learns grammar, facts, reasoning patterns, and more
Training Cost: GPT-4 estimated at $100M+ to train. Requires thousands of GPUs running for months.
Why RLHF Matters: Pre-training creates a 'next-word predictor.' RLHF transforms it into a helpful assistant.

Part 4: The Rise of AI Agents
From Chatbots to Agents
The next evolution of AI
CHATBOT (2022-2023)
Responds to prompts. Human drives all actions. Limited to text.
↓
ASSISTANT WITH TOOLS (2023-2024)
Can search web, run code, access files. Still needs human oversight.
↓
AI AGENT (2024-2025)
Plans and executes multi-step tasks autonomously. Uses tools, handles errors.
The Fundamental Shift
CHATBOT
Human: "What's the weather?"
AI: "I can't check weather."
Human: *googles it*
AGENT
Human: "What's the weather?"
AI: *searches weather API*
AI: "72°F and sunny"
The Key Difference
Agents don't just respond - they take action. They can browse, code, email, and execute tasks in the real world.
Why Now? LLMs got good enough at reasoning to be trusted with multi-step execution.

Goal / Task

↓
Act: Use a tool
↓

Done? No → Loop | Yes → Return
