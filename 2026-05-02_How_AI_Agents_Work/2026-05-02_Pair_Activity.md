## Pair Activity: Be the Agent (10 minutes)

You are going to act out how an AI agent works — by **being** one. No computers needed. Just imagination.

---

### Step 1 — Get into pairs

You will be put into a **Teams breakout room** with one partner. Once you are in your room:

- Decide who plays the **AGENT**
- The other person plays the **USER + TOOL**

---

### Step 2 — Pick ONE task

Pick any one task from this list (or invent your own):

1. I want to save up for a **PS5, new sneakers, or a new phone** — find the current price, figure out how much I need to save **per week** to buy it in 3 months, and suggest where to keep the money safe.
2. Plan a birthday party for 10 friends for under **$50** — pick the food, the activity, and the playlist.
3. Find a chocolate chip cookie recipe that uses **no eggs** and tell me if I have time to bake them before 7pm.
4. Find the next **Warriors home game** and check if the weather will be good for going.
5. Find me a free **Python course** AND a YouTube channel that teaches Python — recommend which one I should start with.
6. My phone is **full** — suggest 3 things I can delete to free up at least 2GB, in what order, and how much space each one would free up.
7. Find the **3 most popular video games** right now (Roblox, Fortnite, Minecraft, etc.), check which are free, and recommend the best one to play with friends.
8. Find when the next **Premier League or MLS soccer match** is this weekend, what time it starts in California, and predict the score based on each team's last 3 games.
9. I have an **algebra test on linear equations** next week — find me a free practice quiz, a YouTube video that explains it well, and build me a 5-day study schedule.

---

### Step 3 — Run the loop together

The **AGENT** must say each step **out loud**, in this order:

1. **"Think:"** — say what you are planning.
2. **"Act:"** — name a tool you would use (web search, calculator, calendar, email, etc.).
3. The **USER plays the tool** — they **make up a fake result** on the spot. Be creative!
4. **"Observe:"** — say what you noticed about the result.
5. Go back to **"Think:"** — what is your next step?
6. Keep looping until the task is done OR 4 minutes is up.

---

### Step 4 — Swap and go again

After 4 minutes, **swap roles** and pick a **new task**. Run the loop again with the other person as the AGENT.

---

### Example (read this before you start)

> **User:** "Find me a no-egg chocolate chip cookie recipe."
>
> **Agent — Think:** "I should search for cookie recipes that do not use eggs."
>
> **Agent — Act:** "I am going to search the web for 'no egg chocolate chip cookies'."
>
> **User (as the tool):** "Here is a recipe — uses applesauce instead of eggs, takes 15 minutes, makes 12 cookies."
>
> **Agent — Observe:** "Okay, applesauce works as a substitute. The recipe takes 15 min. I have my answer."
>
> **Agent — Think:** "Task done. I will give the user the recipe."

---

### Rules

- **Stay in character.** The agent must NARRATE every step out loud.
- **The user/tool MAKES UP results.** Do not actually Google anything — just invent something that sounds reasonable.
- **It is OK if your agent gets stuck or loops.** That happens to real AI agents too. Try to recover.
- **Be ridiculous.** The funnier your fake "tool results," the better.

---

### When you are done

Come back to the main room. We will talk about:

- Whose agent got stuck in a loop?
- Did anyone's tool give a wild fake answer?
- What was the hardest part of being the agent?

# TEACHER REFERENCE (do NOT post the section below)

## Goal

Students *embody* the Think → Act → Observe → Repeat loop by role-playing as an AI agent. By acting it out, the loop stops being a diagram on a slide and becomes something they *feel*.

---

## Logistics in MS Teams

- **Breakout rooms:** Set up rooms of 2 *before* class so you can launch them in one click. Teams lets you preassign or auto-assign.
- **If breakouts misbehave:** Run it whole-class — pick one volunteer pair to perform live, then swap to a second pair for round 2. Less ideal, still works.
- **Time signals:** Send a chat message to all rooms at the 4-minute mark ("Swap roles + pick a new task!") and again at 8 minutes ("Wrap up — coming back in 1 min").
- **Recording:** If you record the session, the breakout audio usually does NOT record — you only get the main room. Mention this to students if it matters.

---

## Wrap-Up Discussion (1 min, back in main room)

Use these to tie the activity back to the lesson and to specific Kahoot questions:

1. **"Whose agent got stuck in a loop?"** → connects to **Kahoot Q10** (agents looping forever).
2. **"Did anyone's 'tool' give a fake or unhelpful result?"** → connects to **Kahoot Q13** (hallucinations) and the live demo.
3. **"What was the hardest part of being the agent?"** → expect: planning multiple steps, deciding when to stop, picking the right tool. Reinforces the chatbot-vs-agent distinction.

---

## Why this works

- **Forces application, not memorization.** Students cannot just *describe* the loop — they have to *do* it.
- **Reveals failure modes naturally.** Agents get stuck, tools return garbage, plans do not work — exactly like real AI agents.
- **Pair format = everyone is active.** No one hides in the background.
- **Funny by accident.** Kids invent ridiculous "tool results" and the agent has to deal with them. The humor reinforces the lesson.
- **Sets up the homework.** When students go to "design their own agent" for homework, they have already practiced thinking through the loop.

---

## Backup if a pair finishes early

Send them a chat message in their breakout room:
> "Now try the same task as a CHATBOT — no tools, no loop, just one shot. Compare: which answer was better? Why?"

This is a mini-version of the live demo and reinforces the chatbot vs. agent contrast one more time.
