==================== SLIDE 4 ====================
What is a Prompt?
A prompt is the text instruction you give to an AI to get a response
Simple:
"What is the weather?"
Better:
"What's the weather forecast for Portland, Oregon this weekend?"
Best:
"Give me a 3-day weather forecast for Portland, Oregon starting Friday. Include highs, lows, and rain chance."
The more specific your prompt, the better the AI's response!

==================== SLIDE 5 ====================
Why Prompts Matter: Same AI, Different Results
Vague Prompt
"Tell me about dogs"
Result: A generic, unfocused response that covers random dog facts. Not useful for any specific purpose.
Specific Prompt
"Explain the top 3 most popular dog breeds for families with kids, including size and temperament"
Result: A focused, helpful response with exactly what you need -- breed names, sizes, and temperaments.
The AI is the same -- YOUR prompt determines the quality of the answer!

==================== SLIDE 6 ====================
The Prompting Framework: R.T.C.F.
R
= Role
Who should the AI be?(teacher, scientist, editor)
T
= Task
What do you want it to do?(explain, write, compare)
C
= Context
What background infodoes it need?
F
= Format
How should the answer look?(list, paragraph, table)
Example using all four:
"You are a history teacher (Role). Explain the causes of WW2 (Task). My students are in 8th grade (Context). Use 5 bullet points (Format)."
Remember: The more parts of RTCF you include, the better the response!

==================== SLIDE 7 ====================
Putting R.T.C.F. Together
Role:
"You are a friendly science teacher for 8th graders."
Task:
"Explain how photosynthesis works."
Context:
"The students have already learned about plant cells."
Format:
"Use 3 bullet points with simple language. Include an analogy."
Combined Prompt:
"You are a friendly science teacher for 8th graders. Explain how photosynthesis works. The students have already learned about plant cells. Use 3 bullet points with simple language. Include an analogy."
You don't need ALL four every time -- but the more you include, the better!

==================== SLIDE 8 ====================
Good vs Bad Prompts: Side by Side
Bad Prompt
Good Prompt
"Write an essay"
→
"Write a 5-paragraph essay about why recycling is important for a 7th grade science class"
"Fix this code"
→
"Find the bug in this Python function that should add two numbers but returns None"
"Help with homework"
→
"Explain how to solve quadratic equations step by step with an example using x² + 5x + 6 = 0"
Specificity is the key to great AI responses!

==================== SLIDE 9 ====================
Prompting Techniques
Zero-Shot
Just ask directly. No examples needed.
Example: "Translate 'hello' to Spanish"
Few-Shot
Give the AI a few examples first so it learns the pattern.
Example: "Happy = positive, Sad = negative, Excited = ?"
Chain-of-Thought
Ask the AI to think step by step.
Example: "Solve this math problem. Show your work step by step."
Remember Few-Shot from last week? GPT-3 surprised everyone by learning from just a few examples!

==================== SLIDE 10 ====================
Chain-of-Thought: Making AI Think Step by Step
Without Chain-of-Thought
"What is 15% tip on $42.50?"
AI response:
"The tip would be $6.50"(Might be wrong -- no work shown!)
With Chain-of-Thought
"What is 15% tip on $42.50? Think step by step."
AI response:
Step 1: 10% of $42.50 = $4.25Step 2: 5% = $4.25 / 2 = $2.125Step 3: 15% = $4.25 + $2.125 = $6.38
Adding 'think step by step' or 'explain your reasoning' makes AI much more accurate!

==================== SLIDE 11 ====================
AI Limitations: Hallucinations
Hallucination = When AI generates false information but presents it confidently
AI might invent fake book titles that don't exist
AI might cite made-up statistics or research papers
AI might give wrong answers to math or logic problems
ALWAYS fact-check important AI responses! AI is confident, not always correct.

==================== SLIDE 12 ====================
More AI Limitations to Know
Knowledge Cutoff
AI doesn't know about recent events after its training date
Bias
AI can reflect biases from its training data
No Real Understanding
AI predicts words -- it doesn't truly 'understand' like humans
Context Window
AI can only process a limited amount of text at once
Understanding limitations makes you a SMARTER AI user!

==================== SLIDE 13 ====================
Using AI Safely & Responsibly
DO's
✓  Fact-check important information
✓  Use AI as a learning helper, not a replacement
✓  Tell teachers when you use AI for assignments
✓  Protect your personal information
DON'T's
✗  Don't share personal info (address, passwords)
✗  Don't copy AI output and claim it as your own
✗  Don't trust AI blindly -- verify important facts
✗  Don't use AI to cheat on tests or assignments
AI is a powerful TOOL -- use it wisely and honestly!
